# Month 1 Curriculum

## Learning Goal

Month 1 takes the learner from basic project communication to a full FinTech delivery update. The learner practices speaking as a delivery/product analyst working with a bank client, internal QA, engineers, product owners, compliance users, and delivery leaders.

The progression is intentionally gradual:

- Week 1: say simple, useful project sentences clearly.
- Week 2: handle UAT issues and status pressure.
- Week 3: explain technical and compliance topics without over-promising.
- Week 4: speak confidently around go-live, stakeholders, and support.

## Week 1: Communication Foundation

Focus:

- Clarify requirements.
- Ask scope questions.
- Summarize meetings.
- Follow up politely.
- Give a weekly client/team recap.

Days:

- Day 1: Client + Team Basics
- Day 2: Clarifying Scope
- Day 3: Meeting Basics
- Day 4: Follow-up Language
- Day 5: Week 1 Review

Expected speaking improvement:

The learner can ask for clarification, identify a client priority, coordinate with a teammate, and report a short daily status to a delivery lead.

## Week 2: UAT and Defect Handling

Focus:

- Confirm UAT readiness.
- Ask for test cases and screenshots.
- Explain issue status.
- Discuss severity, priority, workaround, and ETA.
- Give structured status updates.

Days:

- Day 6: UAT Kickoff
- Day 7: Handling UAT Issues
- Day 8: Defect Triage
- Day 9: Status Updates
- Day 10: Week 2 Review

Expected speaking improvement:

The learner can respond to UAT pressure without panic, ask for evidence, explain impact, and give a concise update on open defects.

## Week 3: Technical and Compliance Communication

Focus:

- Explain data mismatches.
- Discuss API integration.
- Talk about credentials, payloads, and timeouts.
- Handle audit, permission, and approval language.
- Frame new requests as change requests.

Days:

- Day 11: Data Reconciliation
- Day 12: API Integration
- Day 13: Security and Compliance
- Day 14: Change Requests
- Day 15: Week 3 Review

Expected speaking improvement:

The learner can explain technical constraints and compliance risks clearly, while avoiding unsafe commitments.

## Week 4: Go-live and Stakeholder Confidence

Focus:

- Discuss go-live readiness.
- Explain cutover and rollback.
- Prepare stakeholder updates.
- Communicate hypercare support.
- Deliver a full monthly project summary.

Days:

- Day 16: Go-live Planning
- Day 17: Cutover and Rollback
- Day 18: Stakeholder Updates
- Day 19: Post Go-live Support
- Day 20: Month 1 Final Review

Expected speaking improvement:

The learner can summarize project status, risk, decision needs, and post-go-live support in a professional way.

## Content Maintenance Rules

Each day should keep the same five-level rhythm:

- Level 1: introduce or review the core phrase.
- Level 2: listen to a client, teammate, or leader.
- Level 3: speak internally to clarify details.
- Level 4: reply externally or in a client-safe way.
- Level 5: summarize to a manager or do a review.

Each level should include:

- One short NPC line.
- One short micro lesson.
- One target sentence.
- One Chinese hint.
- Three keywords or key phrases.
- One route emoji and one short route label.

Keep target sentences short enough to say on mobile in one breath whenever possible.
